Face Lifting Treatment

Face lifting treatments can include surgical procedures, injectable fillers, and other non-surgical treatments.

Surgical face lifting
Rhytidectomy: A surgical procedure that removes excess fat, tightens muscles, and repositions skin. It can be performed on the face, neck, or both. Incisions: Incisions may be made in the hairline, under the chin, or around the ears. Complications: Possible complications include nerve injury, infection, and anesthesia reaction.

Non-surgical face lifting
Dermal fillers: Injectable fillers can be used to improve the appearance of the face.
Platelet-rich plasma: A treatment that uses the patient's own blood to stimulate collagen production.
Skin resurfacing: Treatments include chemical peels, microdermabrasion, and laser skin resurfacing.

Other face lifting treatments
Neck lift
A procedure that can be performed to tighten and improve the appearance of the neck.
Eyelid surgery
A procedure that can be performed to address other aged features of the face.
Brow lift
A procedure that can be performed to address other aged features of the face.
Face lifts provide longer-lasting and more dramatic results than other facial treatments.
